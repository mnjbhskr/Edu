Bayesian Computation: MCMC in Practice

●●●● Expert

Sampling from distributions we cannot compute analytically

1. Metropolis-Hastings

The chain starts at the current position, proposes a move, and accepts or rejects based on the density ratio. Adjust the proposal variance: too small and the chain mixes slowly; too large and most proposals are rejected.

1.5

2. Gibbs Sampler

For a bivariate normal, we alternate drawing from the full conditionals. The characteristic staircase pattern fills in the joint distribution.

0.70
Gibbs sampling is a special case of Metropolis-Hastings where proposals are always accepted because each draw comes from the exact full conditional distribution.

3. Convergence Diagnostics

Multiple chains from dispersed starting points. The Gelman-Rubin R̂ statistic measures whether between-chain variance exceeds within-chain variance. Values near 1.0 indicate convergence.
