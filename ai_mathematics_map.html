The Mathematics of Intelligence — A Map

From a single number to a system that speaks — the complete mathematical journey

In 2017, eight researchers at Google published a paper titled ‘Attention Is All You Need.’ It described an architecture so elegant that within five years it would power systems capable of passing bar exams, writing code, and composing poetry. The mathematics behind it is not new — it is linear algebra and probability, dressed in a new configuration. This chapter shows you exactly how.

Five Levels of Depth

1. Representation

The mental primitives: how information becomes geometry. Words become vectors, scores become probabilities, and a single neuron learns to draw a line between categories. Everything that follows rests on these three ideas.

2. Learning

How a network discovers structure by descending into valleys. Gradient descent is the engine, backpropagation is the fuel, and the tension between memorisation and generalisation is the central challenge of machine learning.

3. Architecture

The architecture that changed everything. Attention lets a model decide which words matter most. Positional encoding gives order to a parallel machine. Together they form the Transformer — the blueprint behind every modern language model.

4. Language

When architecture meets language: probability over words. A model learns to predict the next token, and from that single objective emerges the ability to summarise, translate, reason, and create. Scaling laws reveal why bigger models are reliably better.

5. Agency

When language models take action. Chain-of-thought reasoning lets models show their working. Retrieval-augmented generation gives them memory. Agentic systems let them plan, use tools, and operate in the world.

Why This Matters

Meaning is geometry. Learning is descent. Language is probability. This chapter covers the complete mathematical journey from a single number to a system that writes, reasons, and acts.
