∞Analysis & Topology

The mathematics of rigour and shape

Analysis formalises the intuitive ideas of calculus — limits, continuity, convergence — replacing hand-waving with epsilon-delta precision. It is the branch of mathematics that asks: does this sequence converge? Does this series sum to a finite value? Can we integrate this function?

Topology, meanwhile, studies the properties of spaces that remain unchanged under continuous deformation. A coffee cup and a doughnut are topologically identical; a Möbius strip has only one side. These ideas may seem playful, but they underpin modern physics, data science, and pure mathematics alike.

Together, analysis and topology form the rigorous backbone of advanced mathematics — from the Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem to measure theory, from complex analysis to category theory.
