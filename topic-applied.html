⚙Applied Mathematics

Differential equations, dynamical systems, and the mathematics of nature

Applied mathematics bridges pure mathematical theory and real-world phenomena. It asks: How do we model complex systems? How can we predict population growth, the spread of disease, or the motion of pendulums? Applied mathematics provides the tools for understanding systems that change over time.

This topic family begins with differential equations (equations relating quantities to their rates of change) and progresses through dynamical systems, chaos theory, and models of natural phenomena. You'll explore the logistic map (simple recursion with complex behaviour), strange attractors (the hidden geometry of chaos), population dynamics (how species interact), and the mathematics of waves, flows, and transformations.

Applied mathematics is essential for physics, engineering, biology, ecology, and climate science. It reveals that simple mathematical principles underlie apparently complex natural phenomena, whilst also showing that tiny changes can lead to dramatically different outcomes—the essence of chaos.

You Should Know First:
Understanding of calculus (derivatives and integration) and basic algebra is essential. Familiarity with the concept of rates of change will help significantly.
