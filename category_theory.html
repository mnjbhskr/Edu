Category Theory

The mathematics of mathematics — objects, arrows, and universal patterns

1. Objects & Morphisms

A category consists of objects (nodes) and morphisms (arrows) between them. Every object has an identity morphism, and morphisms compose associatively. Click two arrows in sequence to see their composition.

Choose a Category

Click an arrow to begin composing morphisms.
Why “abstract nonsense”?

Mathematicians affectionately call category theory “abstract nonsense” because it strips away specific details to reveal the structural patterns common to algebra, topology, logic, and beyond. The power is precisely in that abstraction: a single theorem in category theory can yield results across dozens of fields simultaneously.

2. Functors

A functor F: C → D maps objects to objects and morphisms to morphisms, preserving composition and identities. It is a “structure-preserving map between categories.”

Functor Visualisation

Functor law: F(g ∘ f) = F(g) ∘ F(f) and F(idA) = idF(A)

3. Natural Transformations

A natural transformation η: F ⇒ G is a family of morphisms ηA: F(A) → G(A) such that every “naturality square” commutes. They are the “morphisms between functors.”

4. Universal Properties

A universal property characterises an object by its relationships, not its internal structure. The product A × B is the “best” object with projections to both A and B: any other such object factors through it uniquely.

5. The Yoneda Lemma

The Yoneda lemma says: an object is completely determined by all the arrows pointing into it (or out of it). Formally, the functor Hom(A, –) knows everything about A.

Representable Functors: Hom(A, –)

Select an object to see all morphisms from it — this is its “Yoneda portrait.”
How category theory unifies mathematics

Algebra, topology, and logic all form categories. Functors translate between them. Natural transformations compare translations. Universal properties define objects by what they do, not what they are. The Yoneda lemma makes this philosophy precise: identity is relationship.
