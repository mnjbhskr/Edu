Numerical Analysis

When exact answers are impossible — approximation, interpolation, and computational mathematics

1. Root Finding

Watch three classical methods hunt for the root of f(x) = 0. Bisection is reliable but slow; Newton’s method converges quickly using the tangent line; the secant method approximates the derivative from two points.

Select a function and method, then press Run.

Bisection: O(log n) convergence. Newton: quadratic convergence. Secant: superlinear (~1.618 order).

3. Polynomial Interpolation

Click on the canvas to place data points. The Lagrange interpolating polynomial passes through every point exactly. With many equally spaced points, watch Runge’s phenomenon appear as wild oscillations at the edges — then switch to Chebyshev nodes to tame it.

Click to add points, or use the preset buttons.

Runge’s function 1/(1 + 25x²) on [−1, 1] is the classic example. Chebyshev nodes cluster at the edges, dramatically reducing interpolation error.

4. ODE Solvers Compared

Solve dy/dx = −y (exact solution: y = e−x) using Euler, improved Euler (Heun), and the classic RK4 method. Smaller step sizes improve accuracy — but the methods differ dramatically in how fast their errors decay.

Euler is 1st order (error ~ h). Heun is 2nd order (error ~ h²). RK4 is 4th order (error ~ h&sup4;) — halving h reduces RK4 error by 16×.

5. Floating-Point Arithmetic

Computers represent real numbers in IEEE 754 format: 1 sign bit, 11 exponent bits, and 52 mantissa bits for double precision. Enter a number below to see its binary representation, and explore catastrophic cancellation.

Catastrophic cancellation occurs when subtracting nearly equal numbers. The identity 2sin²(x/2) avoids this for 1 − cos(x) when x is small.
