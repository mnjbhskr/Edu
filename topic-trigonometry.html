∿Trigonometry & Waves

The mathematics of oscillation

Trigonometry connects angles to ratios, circles to waves, and geometry to analysis. It is one of the oldest branches of mathematics, born from the practical needs of astronomy and navigation, yet it underpins the most modern areas of science and engineering.

Starting with the unit circle, you will see how circular motion generates the sine and cosine functions, discover the identities that relate them, and learn to solve any triangle. From there, the subject opens into parametric curves, polar coordinates, and the extraordinary Fourier transform — the tool that decomposes any signal into pure frequencies.

Waves are everywhere: sound, light, radio, quantum mechanics. Mastering trigonometry means mastering the mathematics of oscillation itself.
