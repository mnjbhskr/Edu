🧬Mathematical Biology

How mathematics models the living world

Mathematics underpins our understanding of living systems — from how populations grow and species interact to how neurons fire and diseases spread. Differential equations, probability, and dynamical systems come alive in biology.

This topic family explores the remarkable power of mathematical modelling in the life sciences. You will encounter logistic growth and carrying capacity, the classic predator-prey cycles of Lotka-Volterra, the compartmental models that guide public health responses to epidemics, and the reaction-diffusion equations that explain how a leopard gets its spots.

Whether you are drawn to ecology, neuroscience, genetics, or evolutionary dynamics, these pages show how abstract mathematics becomes a tool for understanding the living world.
