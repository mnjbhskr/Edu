The Map of Mathematics

194 topics, 19 chapters, hundreds of connections — explore the hidden structure of mathematical knowledge

This map uses a force-directed simulation — each topic repels every other, while connections act as springs pulling related topics together. The structure you see is emergent: no positions were assigned by hand. Mathematics organises itself.
The cross-chapter bridges are what make mathematics powerful. A student who sees that Fourier transforms connect trigonometry to image compression, or that eigenvalues link linear algebra to Google’s search algorithm, begins to understand why mathematicians say it is all one subject.
