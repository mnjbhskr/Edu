Magnetohydrodynamic Turbulence

When conducting fluids meet magnetic fields, turbulence takes on a life of its own

1 — What Is MHD? Fluid vs Magnetised Fluid

Ordinary fluid turbulence is already complex. Add a magnetic field to a conducting fluid and the dynamics become far richer: the field can suppress some motions, channel others, and store energy that is later released explosively. 2 — Magnetic Reconnection

When oppositely directed magnetic field lines are pushed together, the field topology can change abruptly — a process called magnetic reconnection. Energy stored in the magnetic field is rapidly converted to kinetic energy and heat. This drives solar flares, substorms in Earth’s magnetosphere, and disruptions in fusion devices.

3 — Energy Evolution

In MHD turbulence, energy continuously exchanges between kinetic and magnetic forms. The plot below tracks both energies from the Section 1 MHD simulation in real time.

Ekinetic = 0.000    Emagnetic = 0.000    Ratio Ek/Em = 0.00
Energy exchange: When kinetic energy dominates, the flow stretches and amplifies the magnetic field (dynamo action). When magnetic energy grows large, the Lorentz force decelerates the flow. In a statistically steady state the two energies reach a fluctuating equipartition — a hallmark of MHD turbulence.

4 — What Have We Learnt?

The MHD Equations
Magnetohydrodynamics couples the Navier–Stokes equations with Maxwell’s equations for a conducting fluid. In the incompressible 2D formulation used above:
∂ω/∂t = −u·∇ω + B·∇J + ν∇²ω
∂A/∂t = −u·∇A + η∇²A
∇²ψ = −ω   (streamfunction–vorticity relation)
u = (∂ψ/∂y, −∂ψ/∂x)
B = (∂A/∂y, −∂A/∂x),   J = −∇²A
Key Quantities
Alfvén speed:   vA = B / √(μ0ρ)
Magnetic Reynolds number:   Rm = UL / η
Lundquist number:   S = vAL / η
When Rm ≫ 1 the magnetic field is “frozen in” to the fluid — field lines are carried along with the flow. Reconnection can only occur where resistive diffusion locally breaks the frozen-in condition.

Where You’ll See This

  • Solar corona and solar flares — magnetic reconnection heats plasma to millions of degrees
  • Tokamak fusion reactors — plasma confinement by magnetic fields; MHD instabilities limit performance
  • Accretion discs — around black holes and neutron stars; the magnetorotational instability drives angular momentum transport
  • Earth’s geodynamo — convection in the liquid iron core generates our planetary magnetic field
  • Aurora borealis — solar-wind plasma interacting with Earth’s magnetosphere via reconnection
