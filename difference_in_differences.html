Difference-in-Differences

●●●● Intermediate

Credible causal estimates from observational panel data

1. Classic 2×2 Design

Treatment group (blue) and control group (grey) before and after an intervention. The dashed line shows the counterfactual — where the treatment group would have been under parallel trends. Drag the control trend slider to violate the assumption.

Parameters

3.0
0.0
Parallel trends assumption: In the absence of treatment, the treatment and control groups would have followed the same trend. This is untestable but can be assessed with pre-treatment data.

2. Event Study Plot

Coefficient estimates at each relative time period. Pre-treatment coefficients near zero support parallel trends. Toggle the violation to see what departures look like.

3. Staggered Adoption

When different units adopt treatment at different times, the standard two-way fixed effects estimator can produce negative weights. This is the Goodman-Bacon decomposition problem.

Controls

Goodman-Bacon (2021): The TWFE DiD estimator is a weighted average of all possible 2×2 DiD estimates. Some weights can be negative when treatment effects vary over time, leading to bias.
