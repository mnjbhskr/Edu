Buttons & Controls

Primary actions use Gold. Navigation uses Navy tint. Destructive actions use Red — but only for genuine destructive operations, never for "wrong answer."

Callout Variants
Discovery callout — used when a learner has found something worth pausing on. Gold left border signals "this is special." Reserved for genuine mathematical insight.
Method callout — Cyan for systematic technique. "Here is how the machine thinks." Used to highlight an algorithm or proof strategy.
⚠ Attention callout — Amber for "read carefully." Edge cases, common misconceptions, things that surprise learners at this point.
04 — Design Principles

The Seven Principles

These are not aesthetic preferences — they are pedagogical commitments expressed through design. Each principle exists because it serves the mission: kindling genuine mathematical curiosity in every person who visits.

PRINCIPLE 01
Colour Carries Meaning
Gold is discovery. Cyan is logic. Emerald is proved. Pink is interesting error. Red is danger. Once a learner has absorbed this vocabulary unconsciously — after two or three pages — the colours do pedagogical work without a word of explanation. Never reassign a colour to a different meaning.
PRINCIPLE 02
The Dark Canvas
Mathematics is vast and dark — but within it, certain ideas glow with extraordinary brightness. The deep space background is not aesthetic fashion. It makes coloured mathematical objects luminous. On white, gold is mustard. On space black, gold glows. The darkness is functional.
PRINCIPLE 03
Touch First, Always
Every interactive element is designed for a finger on a phone screen before it is designed for a cursor. Tap targets minimum 44×44px. No hover-only states. Sliders that respond on a phone. Max should be able to explore compound interest at 9 years old on a tablet without frustration.
PRINCIPLE 04
Immediate Visual Feedback
Every slider move, every button press, every interaction produces an instant visual response. The world changes the moment you touch it. If a learner has to wait — even 100ms — the connection between action and consequence weakens. Interaction latency is a pedagogical problem, not only a technical one.
PRINCIPLE 05
Two Typefaces, Two Registers
Georgia tells the story — warm, human, narrative. Courier New presents the mathematics — precise, monospaced, distinct. The shift in typeface is itself meaningful: "we have moved from story to symbol." This typographic code reinforces the site's core philosophy: mathematics first, then the world it describes.
PRINCIPLE 06
Progressive Revelation
Never show the complete picture immediately. Build it. The sieve crosses out numbers one by one. The compound interest chart grows bar by bar. Watching a mathematical object emerge is itself the experience — not just a delivery mechanism for a result. The journey matters more than the destination displayed all at once.
PRINCIPLE 07
No Empty Praise
The interface does not say "Well done!" or "That's wrong, try again." Celebration is specific and earned — confetti fires when a prime is genuinely discovered, not when any button is pressed. Error states use Pink, never Red, and always invite observation rather than signalling failure. Mathematics is too beautiful to cheapen with generic feedback.
05 — Design Philosophy

Why This Palette?

The compound interest page in the screenshot you shared is elegant — clean sliders, a legible bar chart, clear prose. But its palette belongs to a financial planning tool, not a mathematical adventure. Here is the thinking behind the transition.

Screenshot Palette — "Financial Tool"
Principal · £10,000
Interest · £28,697

Light background, institutional navy, professional green. This communicates: trust me with your money. Excellent for a banking product. Not quite right for sparking a nine-year-old's love of exponential functions.

mathsedu.org Palette — "Mathematical Wonder"
Principal · £10,000
Interest · £28,697 ✦

Dark canvas, luminous gold for the interest earned. The gold glows. It communicates: look what you discovered. The earned interest is not just a number — it is a golden reward for understanding. Mathematics feels like treasure.

The Mission in One Sentence mathsedu.org does not teach mathematics. It reveals that mathematics was always there — in the interest your savings earn, in the shape of a wave, in the way a search engine decides what you see first. The design must feel like a torch being handed to someone in the dark: here, look at this.

The Navy, Gold and dark-canvas palette achieves this. The light-background palette of a financial dashboard does not — not because it is wrong, but because it belongs to a different conversation. Your brand colours are correct. This implementation gives them their proper mathematical home.
