£Quantitative Finance

Pricing derivatives and managing risk through advanced mathematics

Quantitative finance applies advanced mathematics to the pricing and risk management of financial instruments. It combines probability theory, calculus, linear algebra, and computational methods to understand markets, price derivatives, and manage the inevitable risks that accompany investing.

This topic family progresses from fundamental concepts like Brownian motion (random walk) through the famous Black-Scholes model (which revolutionised derivatives pricing) to sophisticated topics like stochastic calculus, risk measures, and the volatility surface. You'll explore how financial markets behave, how to price options, how to manage portfolio risk, and the mathematical foundations that underpin modern finance.

Quantitative finance is a field where mathematics directly impacts the real world—for better or worse. Understanding these concepts is essential for anyone working in finance, and fascinating for anyone curious about how markets operate mathematically.

You Should Know First:
Strong understanding of calculus and probability is essential. Linear algebra and differential equations are helpful for advanced topics. Some familiarity with financial concepts (options, bonds, portfolios) aids understanding.

Suggested Learning Order

  1. Brownian Motion — Understand random processes
  2. Bond Pricing — Learn present value fundamentals
  3. Binomial Option Pricing — See discrete-time option pricing
  4. The Black-Scholes Model — Master the famous formula
  5. Interest Rates — Understand yield curves
  6. Portfolio Theory — Optimise asset allocation
  7. Risk Measures — Quantify portfolio risk
  8. Monte Carlo Methods — Simulate complex scenarios
  9. Stochastic Calculus — Deepen theoretical understanding
  10. The Volatility Surface — Explore market reality
