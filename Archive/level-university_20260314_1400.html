University Undergraduate

Degree-Level Mathematics

Welcome to pure mathematics and its applications at the highest level. At university, you'll encounter abstractions that seemed impossible in school. Group theory reveals the mathematics of symmetry. Vector calculus unifies the physics of fields. Linear algebra becomes the language of all modern applications.

You'll study the Riemann hypothesis, explore topological invariants, learn the mathematics of quantum mechanics, and discover how stochastic calculus models financial markets. This is mathematics as a discipline—rigorous, beautiful, and endlessly applicable.
