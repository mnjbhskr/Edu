∑Probability and Statistics

The mathematics of uncertainty, chance, and inference from data

Probability and statistics provide the mathematical framework for reasoning about uncertainty and making decisions based on incomplete information. These subjects are essential for science, engineering, economics, medicine, and machine learning—anywhere data must be collected, analysed, and used to draw conclusions.

This topic family begins with combinatorics (counting arguments), then progresses to probability distributions, the central limit theorem (perhaps the most important result in statistics), and Bayes' theorem (which formalises how we update beliefs with new evidence). You'll also explore correlation, Markov chains, and statistical inference.

Whether you're designing experiments, testing hypotheses, building predictive models, or simply understanding the world through data, these mathematical tools provide rigorous foundations for reasoning under uncertainty.

You Should Know First:
Basic arithmetic and algebra are sufficient for most topics. Some familiarity with calculus is helpful for understanding continuous distributions and the central limit theorem.

Suggested Learning Order

  1. Combinatorics — Master the art of counting
  2. Probability Distributions — Understand how probability is modelled
  3. The Central Limit Theorem — See why normal distributions are universal
  4. Correlation and Regression — Learn to identify relationships in data
  5. Bayes' Theorem — Formalise how we update beliefs
  6. Law of Large Numbers — Understand the convergence of averages
  7. Markov Chains — Study memoryless processes
