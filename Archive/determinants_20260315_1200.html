2. Geometric Meaning: Scaling and Orientation

The determinant tells you three things: area scaling (how much areas change), orientation (whether handedness flips), and invertibility (det = 0 means the matrix cannot be inverted).

6. Why Rotation Matrices Have det = ±1

R(θ) = [ cosθ   −sinθ ]
        [ sinθ    cosθ ]
det(R) = cos²θ + sin²θ = 1
For a reflection matrix (e.g. across the x-axis):
M = [ 1   0 ]  →  det(M) = −1
    [ 0 −1 ]
Rotations preserve both area and orientation (det = 1). Reflections preserve area but flip orientation (det = −1). Together these form the orthogonal group, where |det| = 1 always.

7. What Have We Learnt?

2×2 determinant:   det = ad − bc  —  equals the signed area of the parallelogram formed by the column vectors.
3×3 determinant:   computed via cofactor expansion  —  equals the signed volume of the parallelepiped formed by the three column vectors.
Sign matters:   positive preserves orientation; negative flips it; zero means the transformation collapses a dimension.
Eigenvalue connection:   the determinant equals the product of all eigenvalues: det(A) = λ₁λ₂…λₙ.
Invertibility:   a matrix is invertible if and only if its determinant is non-zero.
Change of variables:   in multivariable calculus, the Jacobian determinant scales area/volume elements when changing coordinate systems.
