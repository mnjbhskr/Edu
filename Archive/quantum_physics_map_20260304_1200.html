Four Levels of Depth

1. Classical Waves

The starting point: how disturbances propagate through space. Superposition, interference, and the wave equation — the mathematics that Schrödinger would later repurpose for quantum mechanics.

2. Light & Electromagnetism

Maxwell’s equations predict electromagnetic waves travelling at c. Light carries energy in discrete packets E = hf. Spectral lines hint that atoms have quantised internal structure. The classical world begins to crack.

3. Quantum Mechanics

Particles behave as waves. The double-slit experiment forces us to abandon classical trajectories. The Schrödinger equation replaces Newton’s laws for the microscopic world, and energy becomes quantised by boundary conditions.

4. Quantum World

The theory applied: hydrogen energy levels explain spectral lines and the periodic table. Entropy bridges the microscopic quantum world to the macroscopic thermodynamics we experience every day.

Why Quantum Physics Matters

Everything is quantum. The phone in your pocket, the light in this room, the chemical bonds holding your body together — all governed by the Schrödinger equation. Quantum mechanics is not a niche branch of physics; it is the foundation on which all of modern physics, chemistry, and technology rests.
