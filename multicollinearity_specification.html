Multicollinearity & Specification

Correlated regressors, omitted variables and model selection

1 · VIF Explorer

Increase the correlation between X₁ and X₂. Watch how the sampling distribution of β̂₁ widens dramatically. VIF = 1/(1−r²).

0.30
Set correlation and press Run.
VIF₁ = 1 / (1 − R²₁) where R²₁ is from regressing X₁ on X₂.
Var(β̂₁) = σ² / [SST₁ · (1 − R²₁)] — high correlation inflates variance.

2 · Omitted Variable Bias Simulator

True model: Y = β₀ + β₁X₁ + γ₂X₂ + u. Omitting X₂ biases β̂₁ by γ₂ × δ (the auxiliary slope of X₂ on X₁).

1.5
0.50
Set parameters and press Run.
OVB = γ₂ × δ, where δ is the coefficient from regressing the omitted variable (X₂) on the included variable (X₁). The direction of bias depends on the signs of both γ₂ and δ.
