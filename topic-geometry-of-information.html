◈The Geometry of Information

How mathematics shapes every chart, map, network, and scatter plot — and what each representation preserves or destroys

Data is not truth. It is a representation — and the choice of representation changes what you see. Every chart, map, network diagram and scatter plot is a mathematical object with invariants it preserves and distortions it introduces. This chapter teaches you to read those choices with the precision they deserve.

The chapter is built around four questions asked in sequence: what is the mathematical structure of the data (relations, distances, vectors, distributions)? what transformation moves it into a representable space (projection, embedding, Fourier)? what visual encoding communicates that space (position, colour, size, topology, area)? and finally, what pitfalls does the representation hide or distort? Each module follows this arc, whether explicitly or as scaffolding beneath the narrative.

This chapter is explicitly applied mathematics: the same linear algebra, probability, geometry and logic already taught across mathsedu.org, now seen through the lens of representation. Every module cross-links to foundational pages on the site — the Geometry of Information is coherent precisely because it is woven into everything else.
