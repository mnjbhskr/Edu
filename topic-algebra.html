χAlgebra

From solving equations to understanding abstract algebraic structures

Algebra is the language of relationships between quantities. It begins with solving equations—finding the unknowns that satisfy given conditions—but quickly evolves into a study of abstract structures and symmetries. Algebra provides the toolkit for expressing mathematical ideas with precision and generality.

This topic family starts with concrete problems like quadratic equations and extends to abstract algebraic systems: groups, rings, and fields. You'll explore complex numbers (quantities with both real and imaginary parts), the fundamental theorem of algebra (why polynomial equations behave the way they do), and deep structures like Galois theory that reveal hidden symmetries in algebraic equations.

Algebra is both practical and profound: it underlies computer science, cryptography, and physics. But it's also a gateway to pure mathematical thinking, where we focus not on solving specific problems but on understanding general structures and their properties.
