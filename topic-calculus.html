∫Calculus

The mathematics of change, rates, and accumulation

Calculus is the study of continuous change. It answers fundamental questions: How fast is something changing? What is the total accumulation of a quantity? How do we understand motion, growth, and systems that evolve over time? These questions have practical importance in physics, engineering, economics, and biology.

This topic family progresses from the rigorous concept of limits (the foundation of calculus) to derivatives (rates of change), integration (accumulation), and their applications. You'll explore Taylor series (representing functions as infinite polynomials), differential equations (equations involving rates of change), and the multivariable extensions needed for real-world problems.

Calculus connects abstract mathematical theory with practical problem-solving. It's arguably the most powerful and widely applied branch of mathematics, enabling everything from bridge engineering to predicting planetary motion to optimising machine learning models.
