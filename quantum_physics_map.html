Then Planck, Einstein, and Schrödinger shattered the classical worldview entirely. The nineteen pages mapped here trace that arc — from a ball in flight to the wavefunction of an atom.

Five Levels of Depth

1. Mechanics

Where physics begins: projectiles in flight, Newton’s laws of force and acceleration, and the conservation laws that govern every collision and every orbit. These four ideas underpin all of physics.

2. Oscillations & Waves

Energy trading back and forth: springs, pendulums, planetary orbits, and the wave equation. Oscillation is the heartbeat of physics — from a vibrating string to the electromagnetic field.

3. Heat & Fields

Energy in new forms: thermodynamics transforms heat into work, while electric and magnetic fields reach invisibly through space. Forces at a distance — the idea that changed everything.

4. EM & Light

Maxwell’s equations unify electricity and magnetism, predicting electromagnetic waves that travel at the speed of light. Light, radio, X-rays — all the same physics. Special relativity follows from taking the speed of light seriously.

5. Modern Physics

The quantum revolution: particles behave as waves, energy comes in discrete packets, and the Schrödinger equation replaces Newton’s laws at atomic scales. Entropy bridges the microscopic quantum world to our macroscopic experience.

Why Physics Matters

Physics is the science of how the universe works — from a ball thrown in the air to the structure of atoms. Every technology you use, every star you see, every breath you take is governed by the laws explored on these nineteen pages.
