⚛Physics

The mathematics describing forces, motion, energy, and the structure of reality

Physics is the ultimate application of mathematics: it is our best attempt to describe the fundamental laws governing the universe. From the everyday motion of objects to the quantum behaviour of subatomic particles, from electromagnetism to gravity, physics reveals that mathematical laws of profound elegance underlie physical reality.

This topic family spans classical and modern physics. You'll explore Maxwell's equations (unifying electricity and magnetism), special relativity (Einstein's revolution showing space and time are woven together), quantum mechanics (the bizarre rules governing atoms), and statistical mechanics (how microscopic behaviour creates macroscopic phenomena). You'll also encounter applications to chemistry, atoms, nuclei, and fields.

Understanding physics deeply means understanding the mathematics at its heart. The pages in this family reveal how mathematical structures—differential equations, linear algebra, symmetries, fields—capture the deepest principles of reality.
