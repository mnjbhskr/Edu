The Shape of Space · Act III
The Geodesic
✈️

On a flat surface, the shortest path between two points is a straight line. Simple.

But on a curved surface — a sphere, a saddle, a warped spacetime — what does "straight" even mean?

The answer is the geodesic: the path that locally looks straight, even if globally it curves. The path that minimises distance. The path that light follows.

Why flights arc over Greenland

A map of the London-New York flight looks curved — arcing far north over Greenland. But on a sphere, that arc IS the straight line. It's a great circle — a geodesic. The flat map distorts the surface, making straight paths look bent.

Real-world connection — General Relativity

Einstein's key insight was that gravity is not a force — it is the curvature of spacetime. A planet orbiting the Sun, a photon bending around a galaxy, a falling apple — all follow geodesics through curved spacetime. The geometry of space dictates the motion of everything in it.

Geodesics in spacetime

On a sphere, free particles follow great circles. In curved spacetime, free particles follow geodesics too — the straightest possible path through warped space and time. A falling apple, a planet orbiting the sun, a beam of light bending around a star — all are following geodesics. This is Einstein's insight: gravity is not a force. It is the curvature of spacetime itself.

What To Notice
The further apart the cities, the more dramatic the difference between the flat-map route and the geodesic. The savings can be thousands of kilometres. The flat map projection (Mercator) distorts distances — it makes the geodesic look curved when it is actually the straightest path.
Why It Matters
Geodesics are not just for planes. Light follows geodesics through spacetime. GPS signals follow geodesics. Even the orbits of planets are geodesics in the curved spacetime around the Sun. Understanding geodesics is understanding how everything moves through the universe.
Try This

Trace a path along the centre of the Möbius strip. How far do you travel before returning to your starting point? Do you end up on the 'same side' you started on?

What To Notice

The Möbius strip has only ONE side. If you start drawing a line, you traverse the entire surface before returning — proving it's non-orientable. The Klein bottle takes this further: it's a closed surface with no inside or outside.

Why It Matters

Orientability is a fundamental topological property. Non-orientable surfaces appear in physics (electron spin), materials science (conveyor belt designs), and abstract algebra (fibre bundles).

What you learned

A geodesic is the shortest path between two points on a curved surface. On a sphere, geodesics are great circles. Flights arc over Greenland because that IS the shortest path. In curved spacetime, all free objects — including light — follow geodesics. Gravity is not a force pulling things. It is the shape of space telling them where to go.

Coming in Act IV — Einstein's Insight

Newton said masses attract each other through a mysterious "force at a distance." Einstein replaced this entirely: a massive object curves the spacetime around it, and other objects simply follow the geodesics in that curved spacetime. No force. Pure geometry. And it makes predictions that have been confirmed to extraordinary precision.
