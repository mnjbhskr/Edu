The architecture of understanding

💡 The residual stream

Between every layer, the original input is added back: output = input + layer(input). This "residual connection" is why very deep networks can be trained — gradients always have a direct path back to the first layer. Every Journey 5 calculus student understands why this matters: it prevents vanishing gradients.

The Mathematics of Mind · Act III
What the model actually does
🎯

The training objective is deceptively simple: predict the next token. Given every word seen so far, assign a probability to every possible next word. The model is rewarded for assigning high probability to the word that actually appeared.

This is called cross-entropy loss. And it is Shannon entropy in disguise.

💡 Journey 9 — cross-entropy and Shannon entropy

Shannon: the information content of an event with probability p is −log₂(p) bits. The model's training loss is: L = −Σ y_i · log(ŷ_i) — the expected information content of the model's wrong predictions. Minimising this loss means maximising the probability assigned to correct tokens. Minimising surprise. Intelligence as compressed world-model.

Try This

Drag the probability slider below. When P("Paris") is low, the loss is high — the model is surprised. Push the probability up and watch the loss drop. Training is the process of pushing all these losses down simultaneously.

What To Notice

The loss function −log₂(p) has a beautiful property: it is exactly Shannon's information content. A model that assigns probability 1.0 to the correct token has loss 0 — zero surprise. A model that assigns probability 0.01 has loss 6.64 bits — very surprised. Training minimises total surprise.

💡 Why prediction requires understanding

To reliably predict "Paris" after "The capital of France is", the model must have encoded the fact that Paris is France's capital — not as a lookup table, but as a geometric relationship in embedding space. To predict grammatically correct continuations, it must model syntax. Every pattern in language leaves a trace in the model's parameters.

The Mathematics of Mind · Act IV
The limits of the mathematics
🧮

The same mathematics that enables AI also establishes its limits — with precision. These are not opinions about what AI "can't do." They are theorems. Tap each to understand the argument.

Why It Matters

Understanding mathematical limits is not pessimism — it is precision. Knowing exactly where a system must fail is as valuable as knowing where it succeeds. These theorems define the boundary between what AI can achieve and what lies forever beyond reach.

💡 The crucial point

These limits are features, not bugs. A mathematics powerful enough to be complete and consistent, to solve the halting problem, to contain no uncertainty — would not be the mathematics of a world like ours. The limits reflect the shape of reality, not a failure of ingenuity. Gödel and Shannon and Turing are not pessimists. They are mapmakers.
