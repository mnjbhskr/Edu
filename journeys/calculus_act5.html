Calculus in the world
Every field that changes

Every domain in which things change over time, accumulate, or optimise uses calculus. Here is where the mathematics you just learned lives.

💡 Differential equations

Most of calculus in practice is not "find the derivative of x³" — it is differential equations: equations that relate a quantity to its own rate of change. Newton's second law F = ma is one. The heat equation, Schrödinger's equation, the Black-Scholes equation — all are differential equations. They are the engine of modern science and quantitative finance.

Try This

Choose a real-world scenario — falling object, spreading disease, cooling coffee. Which type of differential equation best models it? Experiment with the parameters.

What To Notice

Calculus connects abstract mathematics to physical reality. The same equation can describe a swinging pendulum, an electrical circuit, or a vibrating string. The mathematics doesn't care what the variables represent.

Why It Matters

Calculus was invented to solve real problems — planetary orbits, optimal shapes, fluid flow. Three centuries later, it remains the most widely-used mathematical tool in science and engineering.
