What happens when you type
A sentence becomes a vector

Before a model can do anything with your words, it must convert them into mathematics. The first step: tokenisation. Your sentence is split into tokens — roughly, word-pieces.

💡 Journey 6 — The Shape of Space

This is the geometry from Journey 6. Every token is a point in 768-dimensional space. Words with similar meanings are near each other. The entire vocabulary is a constellation of points — and the model navigates this space to understand your words.

The simplest learner
The perceptron

Before transformers, before deep learning, there was the perceptron (Rosenblatt, 1958). It takes inputs, multiplies each by a weight, sums them, and passes the result through an activation function. Learning means adjusting the weights.

What To Notice

The perceptron computes: output = step(w1·x1 + w2·x2 + bias). When the sum is positive, output = 1. When negative, output = 0. Learning = finding the weights that produce the correct outputs.

Why It Matters

Every neuron in a modern neural network does exactly this: weighted sum, then activation. A transformer with 175 billion parameters is 175 billion of these weights, each adjusted by gradient descent. The perceptron is the atom of artificial intelligence.

What To Notice

The line is defined by the weights. Moving the line = changing the weights. When you found the perfect separation, you did what gradient descent does automatically: search for the weights that minimise errors.

Why It Matters

Deep neural networks stack many of these linear boundaries with nonlinear activations in between, allowing them to carve complex curved decision boundaries. But the principle is always the same: learning is adjusting weights to draw better boundaries.

The architecture
You already know every piece

A transformer is not a single mysterious thing. It is an assembly of mathematical operations — each one corresponding to mathematics you have already studied. Tap each component to see its origin.

💡 The assembly

There is no new mathematics here. There is only familiar mathematics, composed in a new way, trained on an unprecedented scale. The magic is the scale, not the mechanism. And now you can see the mechanism.

What the model learns to do
Prediction requires understanding

The model is trained on one task: predict the next token. That is all. No labelling of facts. No explicit rules. Just: given everything so far, what comes next?

This sounds trivial. It is not. To predict the next word reliably, you must model grammar, facts, context, tone, logic, world knowledge. Everything a good prediction requires, the model learns to represent.

What To Notice

The model does not store facts in a lookup table. It assigns a probability distribution over all possible next tokens. The highest-probability token is the prediction, but the model maintains uncertainty across alternatives.

💡 Journey 9 — The Language of Information

The loss function used to train the model is cross-entropy — which is Shannon entropy applied to predictions. The model is literally minimising surprise in the information-theoretic sense. Intelligence, in this framework, is the capacity to be surprised as rarely as possible.
