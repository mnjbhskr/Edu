What do you do?

The reason
It was never 50/50

Your gut said 50/50. The simulation says 2/3 vs 1/3. Here's exactly why.

When you first picked, your door had a 1/3 chance of being the car. The other two doors together had a 2/3 chance.

Then the host — who knows where the car is — removed a losing door. That 2/3 chance didn't disappear. It all landed on the one remaining door.

All possible outcomes — suppose the car is behind door 1
Switch: wins 2 out of 3 times
Stay: wins 1 out of 3 times
The host's action was not random — it carried information.
It shifted probability. Your gut missed that entirely.
💡 The key insight

When the host opens a goat door, he is not giving you a 50/50 choice. He is giving you the choice between your original 1/3 chance — and the combined 2/3 chance of everything else. Always switch. The simulation confirms what the logic demands.

Try This

Play the game 5 more times. Try always switching, then always staying. Keep a mental tally of wins for each strategy.

What To Notice

Switching wins roughly twice as often as staying. The 100-trial simulation made this precise: ~67% vs ~33%. Your gut said 50/50 — the data disagrees.

Why It Matters

Probability is about information, not counting options. The host's action carried information that shifted the odds. This principle — that new evidence changes what you should believe — is the foundation of all statistical reasoning.
