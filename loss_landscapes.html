Loss Landscapes

The geometry of neural network optimisation

Intermediate

Training a neural network means finding weights that minimise a loss function. That loss function defines a surface — a landscape — over the space of all possible weights. The shape of this landscape determines whether training succeeds or fails, whether it converges quickly or slowly, and why we need sophisticated optimisers rather than simple gradient descent. Here we visualise these landscapes and watch optimisers navigate them.

Too small a learning rate wastes computation. Too large and the optimiser bounces around or even diverges. The ‘Goldilocks zone’ depends on the landscape curvature — which is why adaptive methods like Adam adjust the rate automatically.

3 · Saddle Points vs Local Minima

A 3D wireframe of a saddle surface. Watch how noise and momentum help escape saddle points.

0.10
Hessian eigenvalues at saddle: λ₁ = +1.0, λ₂ = −1.0 — mixed signs indicate a saddle point
In high-dimensional networks, saddle points are far more common than local minima. At a saddle, the loss decreases in some directions and increases in others. SGD noise and momentum both help escape these plateaux.

Adam combines the best of momentum (remembers direction) and RMSProp (adapts to curvature). It is the default optimiser for most deep learning because it works well across diverse landscapes.

5 · What Have We Learnt?

We have explored the geometry that underlies neural network training: the loss landscape. From smooth bowls to treacherous saddle points and narrow valleys, the shape of this surface determines how — and whether — optimisation succeeds.

ConceptKey idea
Loss surfaceThe loss function defines a landscape over weight space; minima are good parameter settings
Learning rateControls step size — too small is slow, too large diverges; adaptive methods adjust automatically
Saddle pointsMore common than local minima in high dimensions; noise and momentum help escape them
SGDStochastic gradient descent — noisy gradients from mini-batches; helps explore the landscape
MomentumAccumulates gradient direction over time; carries the optimiser through flat regions
RMSPropAdapts learning rate per parameter using a running average of squared gradients
AdamCombines momentum and RMSProp; the default choice for most deep learning tasks
