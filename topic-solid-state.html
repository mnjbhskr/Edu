🔷Solid State Physics

The mathematics of crystals, electrons, and quantum matter

Solid state physics applies quantum mechanics and statistical mechanics to understand the properties of crystalline materials — from the band structure of semiconductors to superconductivity and phonons.

This topic family takes you inside the ordered world of crystals, where the periodic arrangement of atoms gives rise to remarkable emergent phenomena. You will learn how Bravais lattices and Miller indices describe crystal geometry, how Fourier analysis connects real-space lattices to diffraction patterns, and how the quantum statistics of fermions and bosons explain the electrical and thermal properties of solids.

From the Debye model of specific heat to the Cooper pairs that enable superconductivity, these pages reveal the elegant mathematics that governs the materials shaping modern technology.
