Matching & Propensity Score Methods

●●●● Advanced

Creating comparable groups when randomisation is not possible

1. Covariate Balance

Before matching, treated and control groups differ on observed covariates. After propensity score matching, the distributions align. The Love plot shows standardised mean differences — values below 0.1 indicate good balance.

2. Propensity Score Distribution

Histograms of the estimated propensity score for treated and control groups. The highlighted region is the common support — scores where both groups have adequate representation.

Common support: Causal effects can only be estimated where the propensity score distributions of treated and control groups overlap. Units outside common support must be trimmed.

3. Matching Animator

For each treated unit, a line is drawn to its matched control. Toggle between matching algorithms to see how the quality of matches changes.
