The Electromagnetic Spectrum

From radio waves to gamma rays — the full range of light beyond what eyes can see

Every colour you see is a tiny sliver of a vast spectrum. Radio waves stretch longer than football pitches; gamma rays are smaller than atomic nuclei. Yet all are the same phenomenon — oscillating electric and magnetic fields travelling at the speed of light. Only the wavelength differs.

3. Photon Energy — E = hf

Energy graph & photon cannon

E = hf = hc/λ   |   h = 6.626 × 10−34 J·s   |   1 eV = 1.602 × 10−19 J
A single gamma-ray photon carries a billion times more energy than a radio photon. That’s why gamma rays can break molecular bonds and radio waves cannot.
Stays constant: Planck’s constant h

The ratio of energy to frequency is the same for every photon in the universe.

    The Unity of Light

    All electromagnetic radiation is the same fundamental phenomenon: coupled oscillating electric and magnetic fields propagating at c. The only difference is wavelength. Our eyes evolved to detect a narrow band (380–700 nm) because that’s where the Sun’s emission peaks. From kilometre radio waves to sub-atomic gamma rays, they all obey the same Maxwell’s equations — the most beautiful unification in physics.
