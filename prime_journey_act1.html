💡 Key Insight

A number is prime if it cannot be arranged into any rectangle except a single row. It has no factor pairs — only 1 × itself.

💡 Euclid's Gift — 300 BCE

Over 2,300 years ago, a Greek mathematician named Euclid proved that primes never run out. His proof is one of the most beautiful arguments in all of mathematics — and you'll explore it in Act II.

🔐 And here's the extraordinary part

Every time you buy something online, prime numbers are protecting your data. The internet's security depends on a simple fact you've just discovered: some numbers are irreducibly lonely.
