Below the separatrix (E < mgL) the pendulum oscillates — closed orbits in phase space. At E = mgL, the separatrix marks the boundary: the pendulum barely reaches the top. Above it the pendulum rotates continuously, tracing open curves.

5 · What Have We Learnt?

We have traced an arc from order to chaos:

  • The simple pendulum is integrable — it has a closed-form period (via elliptic integrals) and its phase portrait shows neat closed orbits separated from rotations by the separatrix.
  • Damping and driving break conservation of energy. Depending on parameters, the system settles into periodic motion, quasi-periodic motion, or enters a chaotic regime where trajectories in phase space trace strange attractors.
  • The double pendulum has four degrees of freedom in phase space and is generically non-integrable. Even tiny perturbations in initial conditions lead to exponentially diverging trajectories — sensitive dependence, the signature of deterministic chaos.

These ideas connect directly to the Lorenz system (weather as a chaotic pendulum analogue), to KAM theory (what survives when you perturb an integrable system), and to the broader programme of nonlinear dynamics. The pendulum is where it all begins.
