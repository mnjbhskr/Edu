2. Coverage Comparison

Compare asymptotic, percentile bootstrap, and BCa bootstrap confidence intervals. In well-behaved large samples they agree; in small or skewed samples they diverge significantly.

BCa (bias-corrected and accelerated): adjusts for both median bias and skewness in the bootstrap distribution. It tends to have better coverage than the percentile method, especially for skewed statistics.

3. Wild Cluster Bootstrap

With few clusters and heteroskedasticity, conventional cluster-robust standard errors have poor coverage. The wild cluster bootstrap restores correct size by resampling residuals with random sign flips.
