Lines, Angles & Parallel Lines

●●●● Beginner

When a line crosses two parallels, eight angles tell one story

1. Types of Angles

The Five Fundamental Angle Types

An angle is formed when two rays share a common endpoint (the vertex). We measure angles in degrees, from 0° to 360°. Every angle falls into one of five categories:

TypeMeasureExample
Acute 0° < θ < 90° A 45° angle in an isosceles right triangle
Right θ = 90° The corner of a rectangle
Obtuse 90° < θ < 180° A 120° angle in a regular hexagon
Straight θ = 180° A straight line — a “flat” angle
Reflex 180° < θ < 360° Going “the long way round”
Every angle has a reflex partner: if an angle measures θ, its reflex is 360° − θ. Together they complete a full turn.

2. Angle Pairs

Special Relationships Between Angles

When lines intersect, the angles formed are not independent — they come in pairs linked by simple arithmetic relationships.

PairDefinitionExample
Complementary Two angles whose sum is 90° 30° + 60° = 90°
Supplementary Two angles whose sum is 180° 110° + 70° = 180°
Vertically opposite When two lines intersect, the angles across from each other are equal ∠1 = ∠3, ∠2 = ∠4
Linear pair Adjacent angles on a straight line sum to 180° ∠1 + ∠2 = 180°

Two intersecting lines — vertically opposite angles are equal, linear pairs sum to 180°

Vertically opposite angles are always equal — no matter the angle of intersection. This follows directly from the linear pair property: if ∠1 + ∠2 = 180° and ∠2 + ∠3 = 180°, then ∠1 = ∠3.

4. Properties of Parallel Lines

CBSE Theorems — Parallel Lines Cut by a Transversal

When a transversal intersects two parallel lines, the following properties hold:

1. Each pair of corresponding angles is equal.
   ∠1 = ∠5, ∠2 = ∠6, ∠3 = ∠7, ∠4 = ∠8

2. Each pair of alternate interior angles is equal.
   ∠3 = ∠6, ∠4 = ∠5

3. Each pair of co-interior angles (same-side interior) is supplementary.
   ∠3 + ∠5 = 180°, ∠4 + ∠6 = 180°

Converse: If a transversal intersects two lines such that any one of these conditions holds, then the two lines are parallel.

▶ Proof that alternate interior angles are equal

Let lines l and m be parallel, cut by transversal t.

Step 1: ∠3 and ∠1 form a linear pair, so ∠3 + ∠1 = 180°
Step 2: ∠1 and ∠5 are corresponding angles (parallel lines), so ∠1 = ∠5
Step 3: ∠5 and ∠6 form a linear pair, so ∠5 + ∠6 = 180°
Step 4: From Steps 1 and 3: ∠3 + ∠1 = ∠5 + ∠6
Step 5: Since ∠1 = ∠5 (Step 2), we get ∠3 = ∠6  ■

The same reasoning shows ∠4 = ∠5. The proof rests on corresponding angles being equal (Euclid’s parallel postulate) and the linear pair axiom.

5. Angle Sum of a Triangle

Drag the Vertices to See the Angle Sum Stay at 180°

Theorem: The sum of the three interior angles of a triangle is always 180°.

Exterior Angle Theorem: An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent (remote) interior angles.

If ∠ACD is the exterior angle at C, then:
∠ACD = ∠A + ∠B
▶ Proof of the angle sum property

Consider triangle ABC. Draw a line through A parallel to BC.

Construction: Through vertex A, draw line PQ ∥ BC.

Step 1: ∠PAB = ∠ABC (alternate interior angles, PQ ∥ BC)
Step 2: ∠QAC = ∠ACB (alternate interior angles, PQ ∥ BC)
Step 3: ∠PAB + ∠BAC + ∠QAC = 180° (angles on a straight line PQ)
Step 4: Substituting from Steps 1 and 2:
   ∠ABC + ∠BAC + ∠ACB = 180°  ■

This proof relies on the existence of a parallel line through a point not on the given line (Playfair’s axiom) and the alternate interior angles theorem.

This property holds only in Euclidean (flat) geometry. On a sphere, triangle angle sums exceed 180°; on a hyperbolic surface, they fall below 180°.

6. Worked Examples

CBSE-Style Problems

Problem 1. Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One pair of alternate interior angles measures (3x + 10)° and (5x − 30)°. Find x and both angles.

Given: Alternate interior angles ⇒ they are equal
Step 1: 3x + 10 = 5x − 30
Step 2: 10 + 30 = 5x − 3x
Step 3: 40 = 2x  ⇒  x = 20
Step 4: Each angle = 3(20) + 10 = 70°
Check: 5(20) − 30 = 70°  ✓

Problem 2. The angles of a triangle are x°, 2x°, and 3x°. Find each angle.

Given: Angle sum property ⇒ x + 2x + 3x = 180°
Step 1: 6x = 180
Step 2: x = 30°
Step 3: The three angles are 30°, 60°, and 90°
Note: This is a 30-60-90 triangle — one of the standard right triangles.

Problem 3. In the figure, two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. If one of the co-interior angles is 65°, find the other.

Given: Co-interior angles are supplementary
Step 1: 65° + other angle = 180°
Step 2: Other angle = 180° − 65° = 115°
