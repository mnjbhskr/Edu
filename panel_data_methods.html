Panel Data: Fixed & Random Effects

Exploiting within-unit variation to control for unobserved heterogeneity

1 · Panel Visualiser

Each colour represents a unit (e.g. firm). Toggle between pooled OLS (one line) and fixed effects (parallel lines with unit-specific intercepts).

—
Pooled OLS ignores unobserved unit-level heterogeneity (e.g. firm quality). If these fixed effects correlate with the regressor, pooled OLS is biased. Fixed effects controls for all time-invariant unobservables by exploiting within-unit variation only.

2 · Within Transformation

Press "Demean" to animate the within transformation: subtracting unit means from each observation. The demeaned data gives the FE estimate.

Press Demean to see the within transformation in action.
Within transformation: ÿᵢₜ = yᵢₜ − ȳᵢ, ẍᵢₜ = xᵢₜ − x̄ᵢ
FE estimator: β̂_FE = Σᵢ Σₜ ẍᵢₜ ÿᵢₜ / Σᵢ Σₜ ẍᵢₜ²

3 · Hausman Test

Toggle whether the unobserved effect (αᵢ) correlates with X. When it does, RE is inconsistent and the Hausman test should reject.

0.00
Set correlation and press Run.
The Hausman test compares FE and RE estimates. Under H₀ (no correlation between αᵢ and X), both are consistent but RE is more efficient. Under the alternative, only FE is consistent.
