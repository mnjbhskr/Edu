Instrumental Variables & 2SLS

When OLS fails, instruments provide identification — if they are strong enough

1 · Two-Stage Walkthrough

Stage 1: Regress endogenous X on instrument Z. Stage 2: Regress Y on fitted X̂. Compare OLS (biased) to IV (consistent).

—
Stage 1: X = π₀ + π₁Z + v  →  X̂ = π̂₀ + π̂₁Z
Stage 2: Y = β₀ + β₁X̂ + error  →  β̂₁_IV is consistent for β₁

2 · Weak Instruments

Adjust the instrument strength (π₁). As π₁ → 0, the first-stage F drops and IV becomes unreliable. Rule of thumb: F > 10.

1.00
Set instrument strength and press Run.
A weak instrument (F < 10) makes IV estimates wildly imprecise and can be more biased than OLS. Always check the first-stage F-statistic before relying on IV.
