🧠Mathematics of Intelligence

Neural networks, deep learning, and the mathematics of artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence—the ability of machines to perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence—has fundamentally transformed the world. Beneath the impressive capabilities of modern AI lies elegant mathematics. Deep learning, which powers modern AI applications, is built on optimisation, linear algebra, probability, and calculus.

This topic family explores the mathematical foundations of artificial intelligence. You'll begin with gradient descent (how we train neural networks), then progress through backpropagation (computing gradients efficiently), activation functions, embeddings (representing objects as vectors), and the revolutionary transformer architecture that powers large language models. You'll encounter loss landscapes, information theory, reinforcement learning, and the scaling laws that govern AI performance.

Understanding these mathematical foundations reveals that AI isn't magical—it's built on principles we can learn and understand. Whether you're interested in building AI systems or understanding their capabilities and limitations, this mathematical knowledge is essential.
