Proof by Contradiction

● ○ ○ ○

Why √2 cannot be a fraction — the first shocking proof

To prove statement P, assume ¬P and derive a logical impossibility. Since contradictions cannot exist, ¬P must be false, so P is true.

Step 1 of 8

The Infinite Descent — watch p/q shrink forever

If √2 = p/q in lowest terms, then p and q are both even — so we can halve them. But halving gives another “lowest terms” fraction, which must also halve… forever. No positive integer can descend without end.

Try This

Can you prove that there is no smallest positive rational number? Start by assuming one exists and see what happens.

Assume there is a smallest positive rational number r. Now consider r/2 — it’s still positive, still rational, but smaller than r. What does that tell you about your assumption?

Where You'll See This

Cryptography relies on proof by contradiction at its foundation. The security of RSA encryption rests on the proven impossibility of efficiently factoring large primes — a result established by contradiction. Every time you make a secure online payment, you are trusting a proof that begins with “Suppose, for contradiction, that a fast factoring algorithm exists…”
