Surface Areas & Volumes of 3D Shapes

●●●● Intermediate

Measuring the skin and the space inside — from cubes to combinations of solids

1. Introduction

Every three-dimensional object has two fundamental measurements: its surface area and its volume.

Surface area is the total area of all the faces (or curved surfaces) that enclose the solid. It answers the question: how much paint do I need to cover the outside?

Volume is the amount of three-dimensional space the solid occupies. It answers: how much water can it hold?

These ideas appear everywhere — in architecture, engineering, packaging design, medicine (dosage depends on body volume), and even cooking. Mastering the formulae for basic solids and learning how to combine them lets you tackle real-world measurement problems with confidence.

Surface area grows with the square of a shape’s linear dimension, while volume grows with the cube. This is why large animals lose heat slowly (low surface-area-to-volume ratio) and why crushed ice melts faster than a single block.

2. Formulae Reference

Standard 3D Shapes

ShapeSurface AreaVolume
Cube (side a) 6a² a³
Cuboid (l, w, h) 2(lw + wh + hl) lwh
Cylinder (r, h) 2πr(r + h) πr²h
Cone (r, h, l) πr(r + l) ⅓πr²h
Sphere (r) 4πr² ⅔πr³
Hemisphere (r) 3πr² ⅔πr³
For a cone, l is the slant height: l = √(r² + h²). The curved surface area is πrl, and the base adds πr². The hemisphere’s total surface area (3πr²) includes the curved surface (2πr²) plus the flat circular base (πr²).

4. Combinations of Solids

Many real-world objects are not simple cubes or spheres — they are combinations of two or more basic solids. A capsule is a cylinder with two hemispheres on the ends. A circus tent is a cylinder topped with a cone. An ice-cream cone with a scoop is a cone plus a hemisphere.

To find the measurements of a combined solid:

Total Volume = Sum of volumes of each part
Total Surface Area = Sum of surface areas − areas of joined faces

When two shapes are joined, the faces that touch are internal — they are no longer on the outside, so we subtract them from the total surface area. Volume, however, simply adds up.

Worked Example: Capsule (Cylinder + 2 Hemispheres)

Given: Cylinder radius r = 3 cm, height h = 10 cm
Volume:
  Vcylinder = πr²h = π × 9 × 10 = 90π
  V2 hemispheres = 2 × ⅔πr³ = ⅔π × 27 = 36π
  Total V = 90π + 36π = 126π ≈ 395.84 cm³
Surface Area:
  Curved SAcylinder = 2πrh = 2π × 3 × 10 = 60π
  Curved SA2 hemispheres = 2 × 2πr² = 4π × 9 = 36π
  Subtract: 2 circular faces (joined) = 2 × πr² = 2π × 9 = 18π
  But the two hemispheres exactly replace the two circular ends!
  Total SA = 60π + 36π = 96π ≈ 301.59 cm²
For a capsule, the two hemisphere bases exactly cancel the two circular ends of the cylinder, so the total surface area is simply the curved surface of the cylinder plus the surface of a full sphere: 2πrh + 4πr².

6. Worked Examples

Example 1: Cone on a Hemisphere

A solid toy is in the shape of a cone mounted on a hemisphere. Both have the same radius r = 7 cm. The total height of the toy is 14.5 cm. Find its total surface area and volume.

▶ Show solution
Given: r = 7 cm, total height = 14.5 cm
Height of cone: h = 14.5 − 7 = 7.5 cm
Slant height: l = √(r² + h²) = √(49 + 56.25) = √105.25 ≈ 10.259 cm
Surface Area:
  Curved SAcone = πrl = π × 7 × 10.259 ≈ 225.81
  Curved SAhemisphere = 2πr² = 2π × 49 ≈ 307.88
  (The base of the cone sits on the flat face of the hemisphere — both are circles of radius 7, so neither is exposed.)
  Total SA ≈ 225.81 + 307.88 = 533.69 cm²
Volume:
  Vcone = ⅓πr²h = ⅓π × 49 × 7.5 ≈ 384.85
  Vhemisphere = ⅔πr³ = ⅔π × 343 ≈ 718.38
  Total V ≈ 384.85 + 718.38 = 1103.23 cm³

Example 2: Tent (Cylinder + Cone)

A tent consists of a cylindrical base of radius 5 m and height 3 m, surmounted by a conical roof of slant height 6 m. Find the total canvas required (curved surfaces only) and the volume of air inside the tent.

▶ Show solution
Given: r = 5 m, cylinder h = 3 m, cone slant l = 6 m
Cone height: hcone = √(l² − r²) = √(36 − 25) = √11 ≈ 3.317 m
Canvas (curved surfaces):
  Curved SAcylinder = 2πrh = 2π × 5 × 3 = 30π ≈ 94.25 m²
  Curved SAcone = πrl = π × 5 × 6 = 30π ≈ 94.25 m²
  Total canvas ≈ 94.25 + 94.25 = 188.50 m²
Volume:
  Vcylinder = πr²h = π × 25 × 3 = 75π ≈ 235.62 m³
  Vcone = ⅓πr²h = ⅓π × 25 × 3.317 ≈ 27.64π ≈ 86.83 m³
  Total V ≈ 235.62 + 86.83 = 322.45 m³
