OLS Assumptions & Gauss-Markov

What happens when the classical assumptions break down?

1 · Assumption Toggles

Toggle each Gauss-Markov assumption on or off, then run 1000 replications to see the effect on the sampling distribution of β̂₁.

Assumptions

Zero conditional mean E(u|X)=0
Homoskedasticity Var(u|X)=σ²
No autocorrelation Cov(uᵢ,uⱼ)=0
Toggle assumptions and press Run.
Violating the zero conditional mean assumption (e.g., an omitted variable correlated with X) causes bias. Violating homoskedasticity or no-autocorrelation does not cause bias but affects efficiency — the estimator is no longer BLUE.

2 · BLUE Comparison

Compare the variance of OLS to a deliberately inefficient (but still linear and unbiased) estimator.

Press the button to compare estimators.
Gauss-Markov: Under MLR.1–MLR.5, OLS is the Best Linear Unbiased Estimator (BLUE).
That is, for any other linear unbiased estimator β̃, Var(β̃) ≥ Var(β̂_OLS).
