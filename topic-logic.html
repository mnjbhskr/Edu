⇒Logic & Proof

The grammar of mathematics

Logic and proof form the foundation of all mathematics — the rules by which mathematical arguments are constructed and verified. Every theorem you have ever encountered rests on a chain of logical deductions, and understanding this chain is what separates intuition from certainty.

From truth tables that map every possible combination of propositions, through the elegant technique of proof by contradiction, to the domino-like power of mathematical induction, these tools equip you to read and write rigorous mathematics.

At the deepest level, logic examines its own limits. Gödel’s incompleteness theorems reveal that no consistent formal system can prove everything about itself — a result that shook the foundations of mathematics in the twentieth century.
