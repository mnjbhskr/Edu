Areas of Circles, Sectors & Segments

●●●● Beginner

Every circle hides an elegant relationship between its radius and area — drag, explore, and see the formulae come alive

1. Introduction

A circle is the set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point called the centre. That constant distance is the radius (r).

Three key measures define every circle:

  • Radius (r) — distance from centre to any point on the circle
  • Diameter (d) — twice the radius, d = 2r
  • Circumference (C) — the perimeter of the circle, C = 2πr
  • Area (A) — the space enclosed, A = πr²
Why does π appear everywhere in circles? The number π ≈ 3.14159… is the ratio of every circle’s circumference to its diameter. It is a universal constant — no matter how large or small the circle, C ÷ d always equals π. This single number connects straight-line measurements (radius) to curved ones (circumference and area).

2. Circle Area & Circumference

The Fundamental Formulae

Circumference:   C = 2πr
Area:   A = πr²
▶ Intuitive derivation: why A = πr²

Imagine slicing the circle into very many thin sectors (like slices of a pizza). Each sector is approximately a thin triangle with height r and a tiny base that is part of the circumference.

Now rearrange all these sectors alternately (point up, point down) to form an approximate rectangle:

Height of rectangle ≈ r
Width of rectangle ≈ half the circumference = πr

Therefore:
Area = height × width = r × πr = πr²

As the number of slices increases, the approximation becomes exact. This is the geometric intuition behind integration — summing infinitely many infinitesimal pieces.

Worked Example

Given: r = 7 cm
Area: A = π × 7² = π × 49 = 154 cm² (using π ≈ 22/7)
Circumference: C = 2 × π × 7 = 44 cm (using π ≈ 22/7)

4. Sectors & Segments

Definitions

A sector is the “pizza slice” region bounded by two radii and the arc between them. A segment is the region between a chord and the arc it subtends.

Sector area = (θ/360) × πr²
Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr

Segment area = Sector area − Triangle area
Triangle area in sector = ½ r² sin(θ)
CBSE examinations typically restrict sector problems to standard angles: 60°, 90°, and 120°, where trigonometric values are exact. For 90°: sin(90°) = 1. For 60°: sin(60°) = √3/2. For 120°: sin(120°) = √3/2.

6. Worked Examples

CBSE-Style Problems

Example 1: Find the area of a sector with r = 14 cm and θ = 90°.

Step 1: Sector area = (θ/360) × πr²
Step 2: = (90/360) × (22/7) × 14²
Step 3: = (1/4) × (22/7) × 196
Step 4: = (1/4) × 616
Result: = 154 cm²

Example 2: Find the area of a segment with r = 21 cm and θ = 120°.

Step 1: Sector area = (120/360) × (22/7) × 21²
Step 2: = (1/3) × (22/7) × 441 = (1/3) × 1386 = 462 cm²
Step 3: Triangle area = ½ × r² × sin(120°)
Step 4: = ½ × 441 × (√3/2)
Step 5: = 441√3 / 4 ≈ 190.96 cm²
Step 6: Segment area = Sector area − Triangle area
Result: = 462 − 190.96 ≈ 271.04 cm²
