Beyond the mean — how effects vary across the entire distribution

1. Quantile Fan

A scatter plot with fitted lines for the 10th, 25th, 50th, 75th, and 90th quantiles. When the error variance depends on X (heteroskedasticity), the quantile lines fan out — OLS misses this entirely.

2. Check Function

Quantile regression minimises an asymmetric loss function. When τ = 0.5 it is the median (equal weights for positive and negative residuals). As τ changes, the loss function tilts.

0.50
The check function: ρτ(u) = u(τ − 1(u < 0)). For τ = 0.9, overprediction is penalised 9× more heavily than underprediction, so the fitted line passes through the upper tail.

3. Coefficient Process

The slope coefficient plotted as a continuous function of τ from 0.05 to 0.95 with bootstrap confidence bands. A flat line means the effect is constant; a rising line means the effect grows in the upper tail.
