→Linear Algebra

Vectors, matrices, transformations, and the mathematics of linear systems

Linear algebra is the mathematics of linear relationships and transformations. It provides the language for describing systems of equations, geometric transformations, and the structure of data. From computer graphics to quantum mechanics to artificial intelligence, linear algebra is indispensable.

This topic family begins with vectors (quantities with both magnitude and direction) and progresses through matrices (arrays of numbers that represent linear transformations). You'll explore eigenvalues and eigenvectors (the special directions in which transformations act simply), the singular value decomposition (a fundamental tool in data science), determinants, and linear transformations themselves.

Linear algebra bridges discrete and continuous mathematics, providing essential tools for physics, engineering, computer science, and machine learning. Understanding linear algebra deeply is essential for modern scientific computing and artificial intelligence.
