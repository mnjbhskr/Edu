Heteroskedasticity & Robust Inference

When variance is not constant — and why robust standard errors save the day

1 · Fan-Shaped Residuals

Under heteroskedasticity, OLS is still unbiased but conventional standard errors are wrong. Robust (Huber-White) standard errors correct this without changing the point estimates.

2 · Monte Carlo: Rejection Rates

Under the null (β₁ = true value), the nominal 5% test should reject 5% of the time. With heteroskedasticity, conventional SEs get it wrong.

Breusch-Pagan: Regress û² on X. Test statistic = n · R² ~ χ²(k) under H₀: homoskedasticity.
