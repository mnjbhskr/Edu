△Geometry

From Euclid's axioms to the shape of the universe itself

Geometry is the study of space, shape, and position. For over two thousand years, Euclid's axioms defined the geometry of the plane and three-dimensional space. But in the 19th century, mathematicians discovered that other consistent geometries exist—spaces where parallel lines meet, or where curvature changes the rules entirely.

This topic family covers both classical Euclidean geometry and its revolutionary extensions. You'll explore the beauty of the Pythagorean theorem, the mysterious golden ratio found throughout nature, the geometry of circles and conic sections, and the mind-bending mathematics of non-Euclidean spaces.

Geometry bridges intuition and proof: it's both a visual subject and a deeply logical one. Whether you're interested in pure mathematics, physics, or the design of the physical world, geometry provides essential insight into how space itself is structured.
